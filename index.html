TCP ex Machina: Computer-Generated Congestion Control

by Keith Winstein and Hari Balakrishnan
MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory

Remy is a computer program that figures out how computers can best cooperate to share a network.

Remy creates end-to-end congestion-control algorithms that plug into the Transmission Control Protocol. These computer-generated algorithms can achieve higher performance and greater fairness than the most sophisticated human-designed schemes.
